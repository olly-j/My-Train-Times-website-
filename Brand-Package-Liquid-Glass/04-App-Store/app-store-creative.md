# TubeBoard App Store Creative

## Listing

App name: TubeBoard: Live Tube Times

Subtitle options:
- Live Tube arrivals, clearly
- The Tube board on iPhone
- London Tube times in seconds

Recommended subtitle: **Live Tube arrivals, clearly**

Promotional text:
Check the next Tube from your favourite stations in a fast platform-board view, with live countdowns, direction grouping, and line-colour clarity.

Short description:
TubeBoard turns live London Tube arrivals into a focused platform-board view for quick station checks.

Full description:
TubeBoard is an independent iPhone app for checking live London Tube arrivals quickly.

Open the board, pick a station, and see upcoming trains in a clear departure-board layout with line colours, grouped directions, favourite stops, and seconds-based countdowns.

Designed for the moments that matter: leaving home, changing lines, walking down to the platform, or checking whether it is worth waiting.

Features:
- Live Tube arrivals where TfL Open Data is available.
- Platform-board style interface.
- Favourite stations for regular journeys.
- Line-colour accents for quick recognition.
- Direction and platform grouping.
- Simple and Detailed board modes.
- Fast, focused design without route-planner clutter.

TubeBoard is independently made and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Transport for London. Live arrival information is powered by TfL Open Data where available.

Keywords:
Tube, London Underground, train times, live arrivals, TfL, Tube times, London travel, platform, station, commute, underground.

What’s New template:
TubeBoard is preparing for launch with a refined platform-board interface, clearer station search, favourite stations, and improved live countdown readability.

App Review notes:
TubeBoard uses TfL Open Data where available and is an independent app. It does not claim official TfL affiliation. Any roadmap features are excluded from launch screenshots unless implemented.

## Screenshot System

General art direction: black glass background, subtle abstract route traces, one real app screen per screenshot, benefit headline at top, concise proof line, amber live data, line-colour accent matched to the shown line.

Export size: iPhone 6.7-inch App Store screenshots at 1290x2796. Keep key headline inside top safe area and avoid tiny UI text in marketing overlays.

### 1. Live Tube Times In Seconds

Purpose: lead with speed and clarity.

Headline: “Know the next Tube in seconds.”

Subheadline: “Live countdowns for the station you actually need.”

Screen state needed: live arrivals list with station, line, direction, and seconds countdown.

Background: deep black, amber dot grid fading behind phone.

### 2. Platform-Board View

Headline: “A real board feel, made for iPhone.”

Subheadline: “Clear rows, directions, and platform context at a glance.”

Screen state needed: board mode with grouped arrivals.

### 3. Pick Line And Station

Headline: “Find stations fast.”

Subheadline: “Choose by line, search by station, then open the board.”

Screen state needed: station picker or search UI.

### 4. Favourite Station Pages

Headline: “Swipe through your regular stops.”

Subheadline: “Keep home, work, and change stations ready.”

Screen state needed: favourites paging UI. Use only if implemented; otherwise mark as roadmap in internal materials.

### 5. Line Status Awareness

Headline: “See disruption before you commit.”

Subheadline: “Line status context beside your departure board.”

Screen state needed: implemented line status screen or compact status banner.

### 6. Simple And Detailed Mode

Headline: “Simple when you’re moving.”

Subheadline: “Detailed when you need the extra context.”

Screen state needed: side-by-side or split mode state, using real UI.

### 7. iOS Ecosystem Features

Headline: “Ready for your iPhone routine.”

Subheadline: “Widgets and Live Activities should appear only after implementation.”

Status: roadmap-only unless the app already ships these features.

### 8. Privacy And Data Source Trust

Headline: “Independent, transparent, useful.”

Subheadline: “Powered by TfL Open Data where available. Not an official TfL app.”

Screen state needed: settings/about/data source screen.

## App Preview Video

15-second script:
1. 0-2s: Lock on black glass board; amber rows wake up.
2. 2-5s: Tap favourite station; board opens instantly.
3. 5-8s: Countdown rows update; line colour accent confirms context.
4. 8-11s: Swipe to another favourite station.
5. 11-13s: Toggle Simple/Detailed.
6. 13-15s: App icon and CTA: “TubeBoard. Live Tube arrivals, clearly.”

30-second script:
1. 0-3s: London night board atmosphere, no official signage.
2. 3-7s: Pick station from search.
3. 7-12s: Live board shows arrivals in seconds.
4. 12-17s: Grouped directions and platform context.
5. 17-21s: Favourite station pages.
6. 21-25s: Line status/trust screen.
7. 25-30s: Icon, App Store CTA, independent/TfL Open Data disclaimer.

Motion direction:
- Glass panels slide on z-depth and settle with subtle spring.
- Amber LED rows tick in place, not with flashy arcade animation.
- Background traces drift slowly and stop during text-heavy moments.
- No fake official signage, no unimplemented UI states.

Export checklist:
- 1080x1920 and App Store preview formats as required.
- Captions readable without audio.
- Final frame contains app name and independent disclaimer where practical.
- Use only real app UI captures for launch.
